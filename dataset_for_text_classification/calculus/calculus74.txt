10.1.1. Definition. A real-valued function \( f \) defined on an interval \( J \) is increasing on \( J \) if \( f(a) \leq f(b) \) whenever \( a, b \in J \) and \( a \leq b \). It is strictly increasing on \( J \) if \( f(a) < f(b) \) whenever \( a, b \in J \) and \( a < b \). The function \( f \) is decreasing on \( J \) if \( f(a) \geq f(b) \) whenever \( a, b \in J \) and \( a \leq b \). It is strictly decreasing on \( J \) if \( f(a) > f(b) \) whenever \( a, b \in J \) and \( a < b \).
