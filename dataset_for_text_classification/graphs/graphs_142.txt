During the Second World War, the Allied army conducts a large-scale operation in the northern region of Libya to sabotage the oil supply of the Nazi military apparatus, an operation known by the code name "Storm of Tobruk", and gain decisive control on the strategic area of ​​North Africa. It is required to determine the overall maximum profit of the allied mission by analyzing in advance the configuration of the enemy defensive system and the assault force of the allied troops and respecting a series of predetermined restrictions.
